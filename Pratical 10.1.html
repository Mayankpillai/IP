MS DHONI

MS Dhoni (Mahendra Singh Dhoni) is a legendary Indian cricketer known for his calm demeanor, sharp cricketing mind, and exceptional leadership. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, Dhoni captained the Indian national cricket team from 2007 to 2016 in limited-overs and until 2014 in Tests. Under his captaincy, India achieved historic successes, including the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, making him the only captain to win all three ICC trophies.

VIRAT KOHLI

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer widely regarded as one of the best batsmen of the modern era. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, Kohli is known for his aggressive playing style, impeccable technique, and consistency across all formats of the game. He captained the Indian cricket team from 2013 to 2022 in various formats, leading them to several significant victories. As of now, he holds numerous records, including the fastest to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Kohli is also celebrated for his fitness and commitment to the sport, influencing a new generation of cricketers in India and globally.

ROHIT SHARMA

Rohit Sharma is an Indian cricketer and one of the most prominent batsmen in international cricket. Known for his elegant batting style and powerful hitting, he holds the record for the highest individual score in a One Day International (ODI) with 264 runs. Sharma is celebrated for his remarkable ability to score double centuries in ODIs, being the only player to achieve this feat three times. As captain of the Indian cricket team in limited-overs formats, he has led India to several significant victories, including the Asia Cup and various bilateral series. Sharma is also a key player in the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he captains the Mumbai Indians and has won multiple championships with them.
